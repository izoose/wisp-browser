Chromium power, a wisp of the memory.

A quiet, dark browser for Windows that puts idle tabs to sleep — so a pile of tabs stops eating your RAM.

The point

Seven idle tabs shouldn't cost seven gigabytes.

Brave was using ~7 GB for a handful of tabs. Wisp runs one shared engine and, when a tab goes quiet, freezes it — then discards it — and wakes it instantly on your next click. Same eight tabs, measured end to end:

Everything you'd expect

Lean, not bare.

A real daily driver — it just leaves out the parts that spy on you or hog your machine.

Tabs that sleep

Background tabs freeze to free their memory, then discard when long-idle — and restore the instant you return.

Native ad-block shield

Ads and trackers blocked at the network level — with a per-site toggle and a live blocked count. YouTube ads skipped too.

Bring your stuff over

Import cookies & logins, passwords, bookmarks and history from Brave, Chrome, Edge, Vivaldi, Opera and more.

Chrome extensions

Install straight from the Chrome Web Store — one “Add to Wisp” button, with popups, side panels and a manager.

A real omnibox

Live search suggestions, history & bookmark matches, inline autocomplete, and keyword shortcuts like yt.

Dark, quiet, private

Dark everything with force-dark for stubborn sites, reader mode, private windows, and a native PDF viewer.

The RAM lever

How a tab goes to sleep.

ACTIVE

You're using it

The foreground tab runs at full speed with all its memory, exactly like any browser.

ASLEEP

You've moved on

After a few minutes in the background the renderer is frozen and its memory reclaimed.

DISCARDED

Long forgotten

Idle much longer and the tab is torn down to almost nothing — then reloads the moment you click it.
